01 — THE PROBLEM

A decision no one can replay is just a guess.

Routing, pricing, and risk systems make thousands of automated calls a second — but when a regulator, a customer, or your own team asks why a request was blocked or a model was downgraded, most stacks have no answer. Logs get rotated, code changes, and the decision can never be reproduced. Without a deterministic, auditable kernel, every automated decision is unaccountable.

02 — HOW IT WORKS

Candidate + policy → decision + proof.

02 · KARAR VER

Allow · downgrade · block

The kernel returns allow, downgrade, retry, or block — the same input always yields the same decision.

03 · KANITLA

Digest + WAL

Every decision is written to an HMAC-SHA256 hash-chained log — replayable and tamper-evident by anyone.
